Subgenre + Pianist Examples

Here are some examples of performances generated by our model for particular pianists and subgenres. These are selected randomly, as opposed to being cherry-picked. We also include several ground truth examples from the held-out test dataset.

All generations are unprompted, meaning the model only sees the associated Genre or Pianist and Start tokens before generation begins. Examples are included both with and without the DPO-P loss.

Note that fine-tuning refers to training the model on the entire jazz dataset with conditioning tokens, as opposed to training only on a specific genre or pianist (just Avant-Garde Jazz, etc.).

All examples are rendered using the BBC Symphony Orchestra Piano library from Spitfire Audio. Note that examples are constrained to 1024 tokens or 15 seconds, whichever occurs first.

The Jazz Transformer at the Köln Concert

These examples show cherry-picked generations for different pianists. The prompt was the associated Pianist token, followed by the opening seconds of Keith Jarrett's famous Köln Concert, which is out of distribution for our entire dataset.

These examples are generated using the fine-tuned model only. We allow generations up to the full 1024 tokens, or until the model sends an End token.
